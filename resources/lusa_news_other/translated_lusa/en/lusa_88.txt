Maputo, 20 November 2020
EYAC // LFS
Covid-19: Mozambican police arrest man accused of falsifying tests
The Police of the Republic of Mozambique (PRM) arrested a man suspected of falsifying Covid-19 tests with negative results, the corporation's spokesperson announced today.
"In this process, we managed to arrest this individual and seize some fake tests, which would be sent to people who had requested them for travel purposes," said Leonel Muchine, spokesman for the Mozambican police in the city of Maputo.
The accused, who confessed to the crime, worked with another individual, who is now on the run, according to authorities.
"We are working to locate his accomplice," said the police spokesperson.
A negative Covid-19 test is mandatory for anyone wanting to leave or enter the country and authorities have reinforced border inspections since the head of state, Filipe Nyusi, announced, on October 29, the resumption of issuing visas. tourism and the end of the need for quarantine for those arriving in the country with a negative test.
Mozambique has a cumulative 14,877 cases of Covid-19, 121 deaths and 13,053 (87%) people reported as recovered.
