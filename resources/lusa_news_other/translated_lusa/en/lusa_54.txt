Portuguese government in solidarity with families of victims of attack in Germany
The Portuguese Government expressed “its solidarity with the families of the victims of the attack that took place today in the city of Trier, Germany”, reiterating its support for the German people and authorities.
Two people died after being run over and several were injured after a vehicle drove into a pedestrian zone in the city of Trier, in southwestern Germany, according to local police.
Through the official Twitter page of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Portuguese Government expressed solidarity “with the families of the victims of the attack” that took place in that German city.
“We wish the injured people a speedy recovery and reiterate our support for the people and authorities of Germany”, states the same publication.
The driver was detained and the vehicle seized, according to a message published by the Trier police on the same social network.
Police and rescue teams asked people to avoid the city center, with even more details about the incident unknown.
Trier is about 200 kilometers west of Frankfurt, close to the border with Luxembourg.
