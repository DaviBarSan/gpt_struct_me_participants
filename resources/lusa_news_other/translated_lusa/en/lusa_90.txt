Porto, 27 November 2020
JGJ // ACG
Domestic violence: Man from Amarante with controlled movements and refugee victim
A man from Amarante accused of mistreating his wife was placed under electronic surveillance and prohibited from approaching the victim, who took refuge in the home of other family members taking their common daughter, the GNR reported today.
In a statement, that police force portrays the defendant, who they detained and took to the Criminal Investigation Court in Penafiel, as a “habitual consumer of alcoholic beverages”.
According to the GNR, the 47-year-old man, “on an almost daily basis, insulted, threatened and physically and psychologically attacked the victim, his 46-year-old wife, in the presence of their youngest daughter”.
In recent months, the statement points out, “there has been an escalation of mistreatment, threats and physical attacks, as a result of which the victim and her daughter had to leave their residence, taking refuge in a family home”.
The Criminal Instruction Court determined the coercive measures of removal from the residence and prohibition of contacting the victim in any form or means.
It also prohibited him from approaching the victim within a radius of 500 meters, imposing control over his movements using an electronic bracelet.
