Covid-19: Opponent of the Government of Mali, Soumaila Cissé, has died
The main opponent of the Government of Mali, Soumaila Cissé, who was held captive for six months by 'jihadists', died today in France, aged 71, due to the new coronavirus, his family and party announced.
"Soumaila Cissé, leader of Mali's opposition, died today in France, where he was transferred for treatment against Covid-19," a family member told Agence France-Presse (AFP).
"I confirm the terrible news. He is dead. His wife, who is in France, confirmed it to me", a member of the Union for the Republic and Democracy (URD) ​​reiterated to AFP.
Malian opposition figure Soumaila Cissé was kidnapped on March 25, while campaigning for legislative elections in the Tumbuktu region (Northwest).
He was released in October, after six months of captivity at the hands of 'jihadists' at the same time as Frenchwoman Sophie Pétronin and two Italians, in exchange for 200 released detainees.
"I did not suffer any violence, neither physical nor verbal," he said after his release.
Soumaila Cissé, a computer engineer, came second in three presidential elections.
