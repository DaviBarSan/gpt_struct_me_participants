Angra do Heroísmo, Azores, November 10, 2020
APE // ROC
Man's body found in rocky area on the coast of Terceira
The body of a man "floating near a rocky area on the east coast of Terceira", in the Azores, was detected on Monday, and the causes of death are unknown, the Maritime Authority announced today.
According to a statement sent to newsrooms, the Local Command of the Maritime Police of Angra do Heroísmo was alerted at 12:00 local time (13:00 in Lisbon) on Monday to "the presence of a man's body floating” in an area public maritime domain.
The authority also added that the information was received through the Public Security Police (PSP) and that the Angra do Heroísmo Maritime Police picket and the health delegate went to the scene, where they confirmed "the presence of the body of a man, of 62 years old, already a corpse."
The body "was removed from the scene by the wide-angle team of the Volunteer Firefighters of Praia da Vitória", as it was found in a "difficult to access area, and was subsequently transported to the Angra do Heroísmo Hospital, where it will be autopsied to determine the causes of death."
