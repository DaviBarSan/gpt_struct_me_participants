GNR seizes more than seven tons of cockles in Valença worth 37,400 euros
The GNR Coastal Control Unit seized in Valença, district of Viana do Castelo, more than seven tons of illegally captured cockles, with an estimated total value of 37,400 euros, that police force reported today.
In a statement sent to newsrooms, the GNR said that a 50-year-old man had been identified and the corresponding administrative offense report had been drawn up for transporting bivalve species in an immature state, with this offense being punishable by a maximum fine of 37,500 euros.
The seizure, carried out by the Coastal Control Unit (UCC) of Matosinhos, occurred on Thursday during a road inspection action aimed at the transport of fresh fish and bivalve molluscs.
"A heavy goods vehicle was detected carrying immature cockles, without the legal measure to be captured and sold, in this case, 2.5 centimeters".
In total, that police force seized 7,400 kilograms of cerastoderma edule, also known as cockle, a type of bivalve saltwater mollusk.
The seized merchandise was returned to its natural habitat due to the specimens being alive.
